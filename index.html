ABOUT



Hello, my name is Francisco Lemus.

If you would, please grace me only a few moments of your time to explain to you very simply why I am 100% secure in saying that you need not look any further if you want the best option and addition to the software development team this position is a part of.

From the ages of 8 to 18 years old, I was very fortunate to be trained with military type discipline in the sports of Track & Field and Cross Country; so much that throughout those years I competed and was ranked nationally in the United States, was league champion multiple times, was awarded league MVP multiple times and led teams to multiple league championships. I also excelled in academics in those years and was placed in AP classes, where I earned all A’s and one B, on average. After this, I attended University of California, Riverside where I completed all prerequisites, up to multi-variable calculus in math, to begin Mechanical Engineering course work. I then decided to go straight into the workforce because I was having to undertake too many student loans. So, I began working in sales at T-Mobile and At&t, where I also succeeded by mastering the art of communication and serving people. Two and a half years ago, I packed a suitcase and moved to New York from California. Here, I was constantly recognized for my performance in America’s top performing district in Brooklyn. Then, as destiny would have it, I arrived again in technology and began my software development journey and became certified in Full Stack Software Development by SACS-CASI accredited Kingsland University.

But, the above is not the main reason why I say I am the best option. First, I know that I have only tapped into the beginning of my potential and I have much to learn. Although my sole focus is to maximize this potential by further mastering myself and everything I do, I also understand that in teams, one plus one equals 3. I understand that I am only a piece of every puzzle I am apart of and even more can be accomplished if I give what I gain. So, I have and do. Some examples are the young men I coached to compete nationally and the one I coached to become champion of the same league I was champion of, the young men and women I have mentored through partnership with Metro World Child, a non-profit organization that serves thousands of children New York City, and all the younger children who have received gifts through the Christmas gift drives I connected through At&t. After a short time, this pattern will manifest the same way in software development with those who enter through the same door after me. The point I want to make by mentioning all this is that I have very strong leadership skills that will be presented quickly as time passes; I know what I know, but the potential of what I will become quickly is my real value because I know that the mastery of everything works the same.

I am a certified Full Stack Software Developer who has mastered and continues to master further the habits and character it takes to perform at a high level, an absolutely excellent communicator and server of people, and last but certainly not least, a person focused on the bigger picture and giving everything necessary in order for the purpose of the team I'm on to come to pass. I don’t know how to fail because of the excellence I demand of myself subconsciously. I only know how to succeed, and will regardless of what I do; this I guarantee.

If the position includes something I don’t know, I can learn it and master it just as easily as what I already know.
